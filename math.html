1. Evaluate the Limits

① \(\displaystyle \lim_{y \to 0} \frac{\sin 3y}{4y}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Use the standard limit \(\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\sin \theta}{\theta} = 1\): \[ \lim_{y \to 0} \frac{\sin 3y}{4y} = \lim_{y \to 0} \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{\sin 3y}{3y} = \frac{3}{4} \cdot 1 = \frac{3}{4} \] Answer: \(\frac{3}{4}\)
② \(\displaystyle \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{2t}{\tan t}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Since \(\tan t = \frac{\sin t}{\cos t}\): \[ \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{2t}{\tan t} = \lim_{t \to 0} \left(2 \cdot \frac{t}{\sin t} \cdot \cos t\right) = 2 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 = 2 \] Answer: \(2\)
③ \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 5x}{\sin 4x}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Divide numerator and denominator by \(x\): \[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{\sin 5x}{x}}{\frac{\sin 4x}{x}} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{5 \cdot \frac{\sin 5x}{5x}}{4 \cdot \frac{\sin 4x}{4x}} = \frac{5}{4} \] Answer: \(\frac{5}{4}\)
④ \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \pi} \sin(x - \sin x)\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
The function is continuous; substitute directly: \[ \sin(\pi - \sin \pi) = \sin(\pi - 0) = 0 \] Answer: \(0\)
⑤ \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{3x - \sin x}{x}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Split the fraction: \[ \lim_{x \to 0} \left(3 - \frac{\sin x}{x}\right) = 3 - 1 = 2 \] Answer: \(2\)
⑥ \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x - \sin x}{x^3}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
This is \(\frac{0}{0}\); apply L'Hôpital's rule twice: \[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{3x^2} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{6x} = \frac{1}{6} \] Answer: \(\frac{1}{6}\)

2. Continuity Conditions

For continuity at a split point, the left‑hand limit, right‑hand limit, and function value must be equal.

① \(f(x) = \begin{cases} a^2x - 2a, & x \ge 2 \\ 12, & x < 2 \end{cases}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
At \(x=2\): \(a^2(2) - 2a = 12\) \[ 2a^2 - 2a - 12 = 0 \implies a^2 - a - 6 = 0 \] \[ (a - 3)(a + 2) = 0 \implies a = 3 \ \text{or} \ a = -2 \] Answer: \(a = 3\) or \(a = -2\)
② \(g(x) = \begin{cases} x, & x < -2 \\ ax^2, & x \ge -2 \end{cases}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
At \(x=-2\): \(-2 = a(-2)^2\) \[ -2 = 4a \implies a = -\frac{1}{2} \] Answer: \(a = -\frac{1}{2}\)

3. Horizontal Asymptotes

① \(f(x) = \dfrac{5x^2 + 8x - 3}{3x^2 + 2}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Degrees equal; take the ratio of leading coefficients: \[ \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} \frac{5x^2 + 8x - 3}{3x^2 + 2} = \frac{5}{3} \] Answer: \(y = \frac{5}{3}\)
② \(f(x) = \dfrac{11x + 2}{2x^3 - 1}\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Denominator degree (3) > numerator degree (1); the limit is 0: \[ \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} \frac{11x + 2}{2x^3 - 1} = 0 \] Answer: \(y = 0\)

4. Find the Derivative

① \(y = 4 - x^2\)
\(\displaystyle y' = -2x\)
② \(g(t) = \dfrac{1}{t^2} = t^{-2}\)
\(\displaystyle g'(t) = -\frac{2}{t^3}\)
③ \(f(x) = \dfrac{x}{2x + 1}\)
Using the quotient rule: \[ f'(x) = \frac{1\cdot(2x+1) - x\cdot 2}{(2x+1)^2} = \frac{1}{(2x+1)^2} \]
④ \(y = (2x + 3)(5x^2 - 4x)\)
Expand first: \(y = 10x^3 + 7x^2 - 12x\) \[ y' = 30x^2 + 14x - 12 \]
⑤ \(y = \dfrac{\sin x}{x}\)
\[ y' = \frac{x\cos x - \sin x}{x^2} \]
⑥ \(y = (5x^3 - x^4)^7\)
Chain rule: \[ y' = 7(5x^3 - x^4)^6 \cdot (15x^2 - 4x^3) \]
⑦ \(y^2 = x\) (implicit)
\[ 2y y' = 1 \implies y' = \frac{1}{2y} \]
⑧ \(x^2 + y^2 = 25\)
\[ 2x + 2y y' = 0 \implies y' = -\frac{x}{y} \]
⑨ \(y^2 = x^2 + \sin(xy)\)
\[ 2y y' = 2x + \cos(xy)\bigl(y + x y'\bigr) \] \[ y' = \frac{2x + y\cos(xy)}{2y - x\cos(xy)} \]

5. Slope of the Tangent Line

Find the slope of \(y = x + \dfrac{9}{x}\) at \(x = 3\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
\[ y' = 1 - \frac{9}{x^2} \] Evaluate at \(x=3\): \[ m = 1 - \frac{9}{9} = 0 \] Answer: slope \(m = 0\) (horizontal tangent)

6. Critical Points and Monotonicity

① \(f(x) = x^3 - 12x - 5\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
\[ f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12 = 3(x-2)(x+2) \] Critical points: \(x = -2,\, 2\).
Sign analysis:
  • \((-\infty, -2)\): \(f'(-3)=15>0\) → increasing
  • \((-2, 2)\): \(f'(0)=-12<0\) → decreasing
  • \((2, \infty)\): \(f'(3)=15>0\) → increasing
Critical: \(x=-2,\ 2\). Increasing: \((-\infty,-2)\cup(2,\infty)\). Decreasing: \((-2,2)\).
② \(f(x) = (x^2 - 3)e^x\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
\[ f'(x) = 2x e^x + (x^2 - 3)e^x = e^x(x^2 + 2x - 3) \] \[ = e^x(x+3)(x-1) \] Since \(e^x>0\), critical points are \(x=-3,\ 1\). Answer: \(x = -3\) and \(x = 1\)

7. Evaluate Sequence Terms (with \(k=1\))

① \(\dfrac{4}{2}\cos(k\pi)\)
\[ 2\cos(\pi) = -2 \] Answer: \(-2\)
② \(\dfrac{5}{2} (-1)^{k+1} \sin\left(\dfrac{\pi}{k}\right)\)
\[ \frac{5}{2}(-1)^{2} \sin(\pi) = \frac{5}{2}\cdot 1 \cdot 0 = 0 \] Answer: \(0\)

8. Definite Integrals

① \(\displaystyle \int_{-4}^{0} \sqrt{16 - x^2}\,dx\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Geometric: quarter circle of radius 4 in the second quadrant: \[ \frac{1}{4}\pi(4)^2 = 4\pi \] Answer: \(4\pi\)
② \(\displaystyle \int_{-2}^{1} |x|\,dx\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Split at 0: \[ \int_{-2}^{0} -x\,dx + \int_{0}^{1} x\,dx = \left[-\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_{-2}^{0} + \left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_{0}^{1} = 2 + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \] Answer: \(\frac{5}{2}\)
③ \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1} (x^3 + 1)^5 (3x^2)\,dx\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Let \(u = x^3 + 1\), then \(du = 3x^2 dx\). Bounds: \(x=0\to u=1\), \(x=1\to u=2\): \[ \int_{1}^{2} u^5\,du = \left[\frac{u^6}{6}\right]_{1}^{2} = \frac{64 - 1}{6} = \frac{63}{6} = \frac{21}{2} \] Answer: \(\frac{21}{2}\)
④ \(\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{1} 3x^2 \sqrt{x^3 + 1}\,dx\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Let \(u = x^3 + 1\), \(du = 3x^2 dx\). Bounds: \(x=-1\to u=0\), \(x=1\to u=2\): \[ \int_{0}^{2} \sqrt{u}\,du = \left[\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2}\right]_{0}^{2} = \frac{2}{3}(2\sqrt{2}) = \frac{4\sqrt{2}}{3} \] Answer: \(\frac{4\sqrt{2}}{3}\)

9. Indefinite Integrals

① \(\displaystyle \int \sqrt{2x + 1}\,dx\)
Let \(u=2x+1,\ du=2dx\): \[ \frac12 \int u^{1/2}\,du = \frac{1}{3}(2x+1)^{3/2} + C \]
② \(\displaystyle \int \sec^2(5t + 1)\cdot 5\,dt\)
Let \(u=5t+1,\ du=5dt\): \[ \int \sec^2 u\,du = \tan(5t+1) + C \]
③ \(\displaystyle \int x^2 e^{x^3}\,dx\)
Let \(u=x^3,\ du=3x^2dx\): \[ \frac13 e^{x^3} + C \]
④ \(\displaystyle \int x \sqrt{2x + 1}\,dx\)
Let \(u=2x+1\), then \(x=\frac{u-1}{2},\ dx=\frac{du}{2}\): \[ \frac14 \int (u^{3/2} - u^{1/2})\,du = \frac{1}{10}(2x+1)^{5/2} - \frac{1}{6}(2x+1)^{3/2} + C \]

10. Area of Bounded Regions

① Bounded by \(x = y^2\) and \(x = y^3\), \(y\in[0,1]\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
Integrate with respect to \(y\) (right minus left): \[ \int_{0}^{1} (y^2 - y^3)\,dy = \left[\frac{y^3}{3} - \frac{y^4}{4}\right]_{0}^{1} = \frac13 - \frac14 = \frac{1}{12} \] Answer: \(\frac{1}{12}\)
② Bounded by \(y=x,\ y=1,\ y=\dfrac{x^2}{4}\) in Q1
▶ Step‑by‑step
Integrate with respect to \(y\): left curve \(x=y\), right curve \(x=2\sqrt{y}\): \[ \int_{0}^{1} (2\sqrt{y} - y)\,dy = \left[\frac43 y^{3/2} - \frac{y^2}{2}\right]_{0}^{1} = \frac43 - \frac12 = \frac{5}{6} \] Answer: \(\frac{5}{6}\)
③ Bounded by \(x = 2y^2-2y\) and \(x = 12y^2-12y^3\), \(y\in[0,1]\)
▶ Step‑by‑step
\[ \int_{0}^{1} \bigl[(12y^2-12y^3) - (2y^2-2y)\bigr]dy = \int_{0}^{1} (-12y^3+10y^2+2y)\,dy \] \[ = \left[-3y^4 + \frac{10}{3}y^3 + y^2\right]_{0}^{1} = -3 + \frac{10}{3} + 1 = \frac{4}{3} \] Answer: \(\frac{4}{3}\)
④ Bounded by \(y=x^2\) and \(x+y=2\) in Q1
▶ Step‑by‑step
Intersection: \(x^2 = 2 - x \implies x=1\) (in Q1). \[ \int_{0}^{1} (2 - x - x^2)\,dx = \left[2x - \frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{x^3}{3}\right]_{0}^{1} = 2 - \frac12 - \frac13 = \frac{7}{6} \] Answer: \(\frac{7}{6}\)
